My Apps

I began programming in 7th grade through an interest in Minecraft mods. While I was unsuccessful in that respect it got me started with learning Java. Over the following summer, I purchased the book Headfirst Java and spent the entire summer focused on learning from it. As a result, I took AP Computer Science the following year and began working in Android development the next summer with a friend and his family.

Poland: a Guide to Major Cities and Jewish Sites

Poland was the first app I worked on and sparked my interest in Android development. I used it as a way to learn about the Android environment and it was my first experience with designing a truly large scale program.

Hockey Marathon App 2015 & 2016

At AIS in Israel there is a yearly event called the hockey marathon where students play hockey for a continuous 36 hours. For the event, along with a friend, we designed an app to make the event even more fluid and enjoyable.

TIMEMUN 2015

I designed an app for the 2015 TIMEMUN conference at AIS Israel. The most enjoyable part of the process was the overhtought easter egg (click on a name seven times and then on the peace dove).

Journey TLV-Your Guide to Tel Aviv

Journey Tel Aviv has been the largest project I've worked on and involved a large amount of work to keep the massive quanitity of data at the user's disposable in an effcient manner.

AssetSource

Working on AssetSource was the first time I had to throughly edit and understand someone else's code. It was a large scale, time consuming process but a good learning experience.

Grinspoon Schools

Grinspoon was a project based on mapping schools in order for shipping services to deliver effectively. It was new for me in the depth of server side communication that was required.

Presidential Elections

While not a particularly complex application, Presidential Elections was a chance for me to combine many of the aspects of Android development that I've learned over the past year and a half.
